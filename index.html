Projects

Below are examples of R shiny applications specifically designed to help researchers explore, understand, and analyze their data without the need to learn a new programming language or download software. All that is required is internet connection to open the link! These hassle-free simulators are tailored specifically for the researcher's primary question of interest and structure of their data set allowing them to quickly crunch numbers and investigate spur of the moment thoughts. Feel free to play with the applications, scroll through tabs, and adjust parameters to see their changes reflected by the tables and charts.

If this is of interest for your research or business, please contact me for more information. Every application is completely customized to suit your needs.

About Me

Passionate healthcare data scientist with a thirst for knowledge. The backbone of my approach consists of needing to understand the "why" behind a process, action, or behavior, posing What if? questions, experimenting with cutting edge techniques and researching a problem from all angles to ultimately tell a story with data. Complimented with a MS in Biostatistics from Columbia University and relevant work experience at a quantitative growth strategy consulting firm, I have sharpened the necessary tools and developed the appropriate analytical thought process to pose questions and offer insight in a meticulous and detail oriented fashion to healthcare researchers, market analysts, and physicians by leveraging the power of data.

Email: hartaigsingh@gmail.com

Music

While my main area of expertise is data analysis and statistics, I am also a dedicated musician with particular interests in jazz guitar and electronic music production. Having a minor in Jazz music, I played guitar for the Stony Brook big band throughout my undergraduate studies. Major influences include Pink Floyd, Judas Priest, Led Zeppelin, Queen, Charlie Christian, Duke Ellington, Art Tatum, Count Basie, Deadmau5, Wolfgang Gartner, ZHU, Tujamo, and EPROM.
