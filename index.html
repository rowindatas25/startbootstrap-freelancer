Survival(Time to Event) Analysis

The goal of such methodology is to determine what are important drivers for the time it takes for an event to occur. In market research an event can defined as adoption of a new product while in healthcare an event can be death from a specific disease. Survival curves indicate the likelihood (y-axis) of the event occurring after a specific point in time (x-axis). Comparing survival curves between two patients or consumer types allows researchers to understand the patient/consumer profile that is at higher risk for the event to occur.

Longitudinal Analysis

The purpose of a longitudinal study is to see changes of a variable of interest over time depending on a subject's attributes. In such analyses, it it important to visualize the change over time for each subject through "spaghetti plots" by specific characteristics such as current product utilization, age, and other relevant attributes. In conjunction with robust longitudinal models, researchers can understand what patient or consumer characteristics are associated with changes of a variable of interest over time.
